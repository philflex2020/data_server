Fast Load Controller — Hardware Schematic

Heltec LoRa32 V2 · 12V→5V buck · 12V→15V boost · LW354 quad op-amp (buffer + gain stage per channel) · firmware: fast_load_control v1.3.3

Stage 1 — Unity-Gain Buffer (OA1, OA3)

Voltage follower isolates ESP32 DAC from load variations on the gain stage input. Output impedance of I²S DAC is not guaranteed; buffer ensures stable drive.

ConfigurationVoltage follower
Gain1× (unity)
Input range0 – 3.3 V
Output range0 – 3.3 V (buffered)
Series R (Rs)100 Ω (DAC protection)

Supply needed≥ 13.5 V → use 15V

LM324/LW354 output swings to within ~1.5V of supply rail, so 15V supply gives ~13.5V max — comfortable headroom above 11.5V.

⚠ GPIO25 LED Conflict

On some Heltec LoRa32 V2 PCB revisions the onboard LED is wired to GPIO25 (DAC1). This loads the DAC output directly and will distort the analog voltage, especially at low setpoints.

SymptomLED glows with DAC output
Fix option 1Desolder onboard LED (0402)
Fix option 2Use GPIO26 for voltage channel

GPIO26 (DAC2) has no LED conflict. Swap DAC1_PIN and DAC2_PIN in firmware if needed, or use GPIO26 as the sole DAC output.
