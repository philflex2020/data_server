Key Findings

  • Noisiest signals — SysCurrent (unit, CV=6.7) and power signals PCS_P--Frequency Response, RTAC meter watts are the most variable. Current near zero produces extreme CV; treat signals with |mean| < 1 separately.
  • Deadband sizing — Power signals (kW, kVAR, MW) need deadbands of hundreds of units; voltage signals <1 V; SOC <0.1%.
  • Frozen sensors — Several boolean/status signals hold the same value for >1,000 consecutive samples — expected for flags that rarely change. Watch for analog signals appearing in this list.
  • Z-score outliers — Outlier readings concentrated in power/control signals during mode transitions; not sensor faults.
  • Physics residual — All residuals negative (mean ≈ −47 V). The linear model V = 1145 + 0.5×SOC overshoots; calibrated estimate is V ≈ 1098 + 0.5×SOC for this site. Residual range −55 V to −17 V over the 3-hour window suggests some SOC-dependent non-linearity.
